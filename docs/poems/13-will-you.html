Will You?

From the collection: Silent Cries And Other Poems

On that Saturday afternoon,
As light showers gently fall,
My eulogy read, perhaps some poems too,
Praises of how kind or cruel I was,
But it won't matter much,
For I'll be on my own,
Quiet and dressed in my finest attire,
A walk in the park, but a knife in my heart,
Gasping for air,
I'll journey to my eternal abode,
As you watch the earth cover me up:
...and the final words will be said...
I won’t hear them, I'll be gone.
That'll be it,
Vanished, with no trace,
But please don't shed tears.
Will you remember me when I'm gone?
