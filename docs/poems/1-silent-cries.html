Silent Cries

From the collection: Silent Cries And Other Poems

In shadows cast, where sorrows reside,
A symphony of anguish, silent cries.
Unheard by ears that wander astray,
Yet echoing within, night and day.

In whispered whispers, a tale untold,
Heartache's masterpiece, in secrets unfold.
The weeping soul, masked by a smile,
Bleeding tears unseen, mile after mile..

Through empty corridors, the echoes chase,
Whispering laments of a hidden grace.
In solitude's embrace, they softly weep,
Silent cries, their secrets they keep.

But let us listen with attentive ears,
To those unspoken sorrows, born of fears.
For within the hushed melodies they bring,
Lies the strength to heal, the power to sing.

So, let us lend a caring ear,
To the silent cries that often appear.
For in the depths of quiet despair,
Empathy can mend, and love repair.
