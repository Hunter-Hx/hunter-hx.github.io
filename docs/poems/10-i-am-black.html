I Am Black

From the collection: Silent Cries And Other Poems

I am black,
My tears flow blackened,
And my vision dark,
Everything in me, obscured,
So, they say –
I’m filled with sadness.

I am black,
A primitive creature to some,
But with an undeniable history,
And yes, I’m black,
My culture, unique,
And my thirst for knowledge, insatiable,
I am black.

I am black,
With a heart as dark as coal, they claim,
But red blood runs through my veins,
And they judge me from my skin,
Yes, I’m a child of the blacks,
I am black.

I am black,
My mind misunderstood,
But soon I’ll make them understand,
Yes, I am black.
I am black,
And I won’t apologize for it,
Though sometimes I wish I were green,
How would they treat me then?
Like grass?
