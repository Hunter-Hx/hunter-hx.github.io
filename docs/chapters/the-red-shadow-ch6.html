Chapter Six

I Don't Know

Fiction ‚Ä¢ 5 min read ‚Ä¢ Updated Feb 2026

Six days had passed and I planned to travel back to where I had lost Mutinda. With him, I could easily find Wairimu and with that, I was set for a new life, maybe. Since I had quite some cash, I was on.

All those six days, I was at friend's place, Mango. A hospitable friend except his mom. My friends always had problems with their moms. They never liked me and I never cared to even think of it any minute. I always trolled.

I picked my small briefcase which I had packed some clothes and matched out. I walked some distance and bordered a taxi to Nyanyi, a small market where most of lost people were often found. I thought Mutinda would be there but he wasn't. Where was he? And Wairimu? And the Holy boys? I didn't know where any of them were at that very moment and I guess they didn't know where I was either. I thought now it was time to forget my friend and move on with life. I thought maybe he was living a good life and so I never wanted to interfere having gave him a wife. Within the market, I picked some stuff to eat and decided to go back to Dayo feigning a visit with the obvious motive; talk to Wairimu.

I took another vehicle that let towards Mutinda's home so I'd take the route we used the previous day we met Dayo. Within the van, I saw someone that looked like one of the holy boys. I took out my phone slowly and checked a group photo that we had taken earlier and for sure, it was him, Majengo, the quietest of the crew. I quickly pulled over and alighted not to be seen. This was now the fourth time they were almost catching me. I had to find a safe way.

I walked the distance to Dayo's home. It took me about four hours to reach there. As I approached the gate, I saw someone standing there. It was Dayo. She saw me and I could see her smile. She was happy to see me.

'Grandson, you came back,' she said as she hugged me.
'Yeah, I just missed you and Wairimu,' I lied.
'Ooh, she's inside, go and see her,' she said.

I walked in and there she was, Wairimu, busy doing some laundry. She saw me and for a minute she was stunned. She didn't expect me to be back. I walked to her and helped her with the bucket of water.

'You came back?' she asked smiling.
'Yeah, I'm back for you,' I said.
'Ooh, I missed you too Peter,' she said.

The afternoon went on well and I told her all my struggles. I didn't hide anything. I told her about the holy boys and Melisa. She was shocked but she promised to keep it a secret. I felt a relief having told someone what I was going through.

As evening approached, I asked to use the washroom. Inside, I looked at myself on the mirror and I saw a different person. I was no longer the church boy. I was a hunter. I was on the run. I had to make a choice; to stay or to move on. I chose to stay with Wairimu and Dayo for a while.

After finishing my business, I felt like I was being watched. I tried to be calm but it was hard to. I heard footsteps and then, 'Hey, is everything okey?'

'Everything is fine, just had a running stomach and now I'm good,' I said as I walked out, toilet's door slamming behind me.
'It's so weird to be in here, right?' Wairimu started.
'Nope, its fine. Its full of serenity everyone needs.'
'Things aren't always what they seem to be,' Wairimu said making a step ahead.
'What do you mean?'
'I'll tell you a story one day. Dayo needs me, lemme go help her.' She walked as a wave of suspense hovered around me. Things aren't always what they seem to be. What did she mean? Was she suffering here? I hope she wasn't.

It had been three hours and I still couldn't say what brought me to them. I was ashamed of asking for help. A place to sleep and how to find my friend. I had heard that medicine men and women stayed in the forest and somehow, they helped, a lot. I was now going to ask for that help.

Before I could walk into the kitchen and ask for what brought me, Wairimu happened to have been coming my direction.

'What did you mean by what you said.'
'Nothing, I just said it.'
'No. You are lying.'
'Yes, I am. Dayo knows you are lying and she won't tell you unless you open up,' she said covering her eyes from the cold of the blowing wind.
'Well, I'm going to tell her. Wanna come with me?'

'Nah! It's your burden,' she said smiling headed the opposite direction.
